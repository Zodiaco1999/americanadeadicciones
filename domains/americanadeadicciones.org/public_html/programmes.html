Goals

Provide and guide parents, young people and teachers a series of neuropsychological strategies to strengthen parenting styles and family communication through affective dialogue and negotiation, to reduce the incidence of early contact with marijuana or other drugs.

Statistics on consumption in the school environment

The most consumed illicit substance in Colombia is marijuana. Of the total number of consumers of illicit drugs, 87% are marijuana users. Approximately 62% of marijuana users are in school ages ranging from 12 to 24 years, this evaluated by the Drug Observatory of Colombia in 2017.


Goals

Improve worker productivity by promoting mental health in the organization.

Mental Health at Work Program

Statistics on consumption in the workplace

In recent years about 53,000 people who were involved in a work accident had consumed some psychoactive substance 12 hours before the incident. 92,582 employees excused themselves from attending work at least one day in the month as a result of the use of drugs or alcohol. Study conducted by the Ministries of Justice and Health, the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (Unodc) and the OAS.

Mind-Free Program

Goals

Provide adolescents processed by the SRPA (Responsibility System for Adolescents) psychological tools to increase the motivation of achievement through resilience, to modify their behaviors related to the abuse of psychoactive substances, increase their abilities to lead a healthy life, in order to direct adolescents in the planning and preparation of their life project away from crime and drug use.

Statistics on consumption in the SRPA

By 2016, according to data from the ICBF (Colombian Institute for Family Welfare), 22,198 adolescents were admitted to the SRPA (Responsibility System for Adolescents), of which 87.15% (19,346) were men and 12.85% women. 60% of adolescents prosecuted by the SRPA adolescent criminal responsibility system report having committed the crime under the influence of some psychoactive substance; 66.5% say they would not have committed the crime if they had not consumed; and 23.8% say they committed the crime to buy SPA. Colombia Drug Observatory 2017.


Goals

Reduce harm from drug use in persons deprived of liberty in prisons.

Educate yourself and get rid of Drugs Program

Consumption statistics in prisons

In relation to the right to health, the prevalence of drug use in prisons amounts to 60% (UNODC, 2016), as well as other diseases such as HIV and tuberculosis are up to 20 times higher behind bars. Only 73 of the 138 prisons in the country have medical personnel for intramural care.
